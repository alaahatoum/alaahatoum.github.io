The Wave Energy Converter project focused on developing a mooring system prototype capable of maximizing energy extraction while minimizing wave interference effects. By relocating a secondary converter line, our team sought to optimize the energy capture of the buoy array and enhance overall system efficiency.

My contributions began with developing a sensor suite to capture precise buoy motion data. This system fed real-time information into a machine learning algorithm that predicted the location of the next peak wave, enabling smarter control of the mooring system.

I assisted in the fabrication and assembly of both the sensor suite and mechanical mooring prototype, ensuring accurate integration and reliable performance under simulated wave conditions. Calibration procedures were established and iteratively refined to maintain sensor accuracy across variable environmental conditions.

To validate system performance, I created testing protocols using wave simulation experiments, collecting empirical data to verify predictive accuracy. By analyzing sensor data, I identified causes of incorrect buoy motion and contributed to design improvements that enhanced reliability.

Additionally, I authored detailed technical documentation outlining testing methodologies, data analysis, and iterative design enhancements. Throughout the project, I collaborated with a multidisciplinary team, including software, electrical, and mechanical engineers, to ensure that the mooring system met stringent performance requirements.
