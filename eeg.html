EEG Headset Design

Modular, user-friendly EEG headset for flexible neuroscience research applications

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

As the Headset Design Lead at NeurotechUofT, I led a multi-year project to develop a modular, user-friendly EEG headset. Our goal was to create a system capable of flexible neuroscience research across multiple users, providing precise electrode placement and maximum comfort.

I designed the headset in Fusion360 and SolidWorks, ensuring it was fully 3D-printable and capable of rapid reconfiguration without tools. The snap-fit electrode holder, combined with spring-loaded electrodes, allowed firm, stable contact with the scalp, while the rail movement guide and headset tightening mechanism provided structural stability and alignment. Components were marked with distance and angle indicators to assist accurate electrode placement, feeding into a program for targeted brain region exploration.

Prototyping involved FDM 3D printing using TPU, PLA, and PETG, with iterative testing to evaluate durability, flexibility, and ease of use. I developed testing protocols simulating real-world conditions, gathering data to inform improvements in ergonomics and mechanical performance.

Leading a team of six engineering students, I coordinated timelines, assigned tasks, and collaborated with neuroscientists, electronics engineers, and biomedical researchers. Through repeated assembly, testing, and data collection, we refined the headset to be a reliable, user-centric tool for flexible EEG research applications.
